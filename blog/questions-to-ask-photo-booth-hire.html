Photo Booth Hire: 15 Questions to Ask Before You Book

Hiring a photo booth for your wedding, party, or corporate event should be exciting — not stressful. But with dozens of photo booth companies operating across Ireland, how do you tell the professionals from the chancers? The answer is simple: ask the right questions.

We have put together this list of 15 essential questions to ask before booking a photo booth. Whether it is your first time hiring one or you are comparing quotes, these questions will help you make a confident, informed decision and avoid any nasty surprises on the day.

The 15 Essential Questions

1. What types of photo booth do you offer?

Not all photo booths are the same. Some companies offer a single booth style, whilst others provide a full range. The type of booth you choose should match your event style, venue, and guest count. Ask the company to walk you through their options — from open air booths and enclosed booths to 360 video booths, selfie mirrors, and more specialist options like magazine booths or AI booths. A good company will recommend the best fit based on your needs, not just upsell you on the most expensive option.

2. What’s included in the price?

This is the single most important question. A headline price means nothing unless you know exactly what it covers. Ask specifically about: delivery and collection, setup and pack-down, a professional booth attendant, unlimited prints, digital copies of all photos, custom overlays or templates, props, and a guest-facing sharing station. If any of these are listed as “add-ons,” you need to factor those costs into the real price.

3. How long is the hire period?

Most photo booth hire packages in Ireland run for 2–4 hours. Check whether the hire time includes setup and pack-down or just active booth time. Also ask about overtime rates — if the party is going well, you may want to extend. Knowing the cost upfront saves awkward conversations on the night.

4. Are there any hidden fees?

Travel charges, fuel surcharges, setup fees, late-night premiums — these can add up fast. A reputable company will be transparent about all costs from the outset. Ask directly: “Is the price you have quoted me the final price, or are there additional charges I should know about?”

5. What camera equipment do you use?

This question separates the professionals from the amateurs. There is an enormous quality difference between a booth using a professional DSLR camera with studio lighting and one using a webcam or tablet camera. Ask to see sample photos from actual events. If the images look grainy, poorly lit, or washed out, that is exactly what your guests will receive.

6. Can we customise the photo overlay or template?

Customisation turns a generic photo booth into a personalised experience. For weddings, you might want your names and date on every print. For corporate events, your company logo and branding. For birthdays, a themed design. Ask what level of customisation is included and whether there is an additional design fee.

7. How do guests get their photos?

Modern photo booths should offer multiple delivery methods. Physical prints are still popular (instant gratification never goes out of style), but digital sharing is essential. Ask about QR code sharing, email delivery, SMS options, and whether there is an online gallery or event microsite where guests can view and download all photos after the event.

8. What space and power do you need?

Most photo booths require a 2–3 metre area and access to a standard electrical outlet. However, some larger setups (particularly 360 booths and enclosed booths) need more space. Share your venue details with the company and ask them to confirm whether their equipment will fit comfortably. A professional company will ask about your venue before you even have to raise this question.

9. Do you provide a booth attendant?

This is non-negotiable. A professional attendant ensures the booth runs smoothly, helps guests with props and posing, troubleshoots any issues in real time, and keeps the queue moving. Some budget operators drop off a booth and leave — this almost always leads to problems. Always confirm that a dedicated attendant is included for the entire hire period.

10. What happens if there’s a technical issue?

Technology can be unpredictable. Ask the company about their contingency plan. Do they carry backup equipment? How quickly can they resolve a fault? A reliable company will have backup cameras, printers, and laptops on hand. Our team at Photobooth Guys carries full backup equipment to every event — we have never had a booking where the booth was down for more than a few minutes.

11. Do you need WiFi?

Many venues have unreliable WiFi, and some have none at all. Ask whether the photo booth company carries their own mobile internet connection. A good company will use 4G or 5G mobile broadband so that digital sharing works seamlessly regardless of the venue’s connectivity. If a company relies entirely on venue WiFi, that is a risk worth noting.

12. Can we see reviews or testimonials?

Social proof matters. Ask for links to Google reviews, Facebook reviews, or testimonials from past clients. Look for reviews that mention specific events similar to yours. A company with consistently positive, detailed reviews is a far safer bet than one with a handful of vague five-star ratings. Check whether they have photos or videos from real events on their social media.

13. What’s your cancellation or rescheduling policy?

Life happens. Venues change, dates shift, and sometimes events are cancelled altogether. Before you pay a deposit, understand the company’s cancellation and rescheduling terms. Is the deposit refundable? Can you reschedule without penalty? What notice period is required? A fair policy protects both parties and shows the company operates professionally.

14. Do you offer multi-booth packages?

For larger events, having two or more booths can dramatically improve the guest experience and reduce waiting times. Many companies offer discounted rates when you book multiple booths for the same event. If you are planning a wedding with 200+ guests or a corporate event with multiple zones, ask about package pricing — you could save significantly compared to booking each booth individually.

15. How far in advance should we book?

Peak season in Ireland runs from May to September for weddings and October to December for corporate events. During these periods, the best photo booth companies book out weeks or even months in advance. As a general rule, aim to book 4–8 weeks ahead. For peak-season Saturdays, 2–3 months is safer. If your date is flexible, you may have more options — but do not leave it to the last minute.

Red Flags to Watch For

Asking the right questions is important, but knowing the warning signs is equally valuable. Be cautious if you encounter any of the following:

No attendant included. A “drop off and collect” service is a recipe for problems. If something goes wrong, there is nobody there to fix it.

Webcam or tablet-quality images. If the company cannot show you sharp, well-lit sample photos taken with professional equipment, your guests will be disappointed with the results.

No customisation options. A generic, unbranded booth adds very little to your event. Customisation should be standard, not a premium add-on.

No public liability insurance. Any company setting up equipment at your venue should carry appropriate insurance. If they do not, your venue may refuse entry — or you could be liable if something goes wrong.

Evasive answers about pricing. If a company will not give you a clear, all-inclusive price, there are almost certainly additional charges waiting to surprise you.

Selfie Mirror vs Photo Booth: Which Is Right for Your Wedding?

If you are planning a wedding in Ireland and want to give your guests an unforgettable experience, you have probably come across two popular options: a selfie mirror and a photo booth. Both are brilliant choices for wedding entertainment, but they offer quite different vibes. So which one is right for your big day?

We have set up hundreds of selfie mirrors and photo booths at weddings across Ireland — from intimate castle celebrations in Westmeath to grand ballroom receptions in Dublin. In this guide, we will break down the key differences so you can make the perfect choice for your wedding.

What Is a Selfie Mirror?

A selfie mirror is a full-length interactive mirror that looks like an elegant piece of decor but hides some seriously impressive technology. When your guests step in front of it, the mirror comes alive with animated prompts, touchscreen games, digital signatures, and — of course — a high-quality camera that captures stunning photos.

The experience is wonderfully immersive. Guests can interact directly with the mirror's touchscreen, add digital stamps and emojis to their photos, sign their names on the screen, and receive instant prints within seconds. It feels like something from the future, wrapped in a sleek, stylish frame that suits even the most elegant wedding venue.

What Is an Open Air Photo Booth?

An open air photo booth is the modern evolution of the classic photo booth experience. Instead of squeezing into an enclosed box, guests stand in front of a professional backdrop with studio-quality lighting. A DSLR camera captures every shot, producing print-quality images that your guests will treasure for years.

The open design means larger groups can jump in together — perfect for those big family photos and squad shots. Add in a collection of fun props, unlimited instant prints, and a digital gallery, and you have got a recipe for non-stop entertainment all night long.

Side-by-Side Comparison: Selfie Mirror vs Photo Booth

Let us get into the details that matter most when choosing between a selfie mirror and a photo booth for your wedding.

Photo Quality

Both options deliver excellent photo quality. Our open air photo booths and selfie mirrors use professional DSLR cameras with studio-grade lighting, so you can expect sharp, well-lit images every time. The selfie mirror does have one extra trick up its sleeve — beauty-enhancing filters that subtly smooth skin tones and brighten features, giving every guest that red-carpet glow.

Guest Experience

This is where the two really differ. The selfie mirror is more interactive, thanks to its touchscreen interface. Guests can play mini-games, draw on the screen, and navigate animated prompts that guide them through the experience. It is engaging, modern, and always gets people talking.

The photo booth is more classic and relaxed. Guests grab some props, strike a pose, and the photos are taken automatically. It is effortless fun that everyone — from grandparents to toddlers — can enjoy without any learning curve.

Group Sizes

If your family is anything like most Irish families, you will want plenty of room for group shots. The open air photo booth comfortably handles larger groups of 8 to 10 people (or more if everyone squeezes in). The selfie mirror is best suited for smaller groups of 1 to 4 people, as the mirror frame naturally focuses on a tighter area.

Space Requirements

Both options need roughly the same amount of space — approximately 2 to 3 metres by 2 to 3 metres. This includes room for the equipment, backdrop (for the photo booth), and enough space for guests to pose comfortably. Most Irish wedding venues can accommodate either option without any trouble.

Prints

Both the selfie mirror and photo booth come with unlimited instant prints as standard. Every guest receives a high-quality printed photo within seconds of posing. You also get a full digital gallery of every image from the night, which makes a wonderful keepsake to browse through after the honeymoon.

Cost

Budget is always a consideration when planning a wedding. Our open air photo booth starts from €680, whilst our selfie mirror starts from €730. Both represent exceptional value when you consider the hours of entertainment, unlimited prints, and digital gallery included.

Best For

The selfie mirror is ideal for elegant, glamorous weddings where you want a sophisticated entertainment option that doubles as a talking point. Think black-tie affairs, boutique hotel receptions, and venues with a contemporary aesthetic.

The photo booth suits fun, relaxed weddings where the focus is on getting everyone involved and creating hilarious group shots. Think country house celebrations, barn weddings, and any reception where the craic is mighty.

How to Decide: Questions to Ask Yourself

Still not sure which option is right for your wedding? Ask yourself these questions:

What is the vibe of your wedding? Elegant and sophisticated leans towards a selfie mirror. Fun and relaxed suits a photo booth. Glamorous and Instagram-worthy? Consider the Glam Booth or 360 Booth.

How important are group shots? If your guest list includes large families and friend groups who will want to pile in together, the open air photo booth is the better choice.

Do your guests love technology? Tech-savvy guests will adore the interactive touchscreen experience of the selfie mirror. If your guest list skews older or more traditional, the straightforward photo booth might be a safer bet.

What does your venue look like? Consider the aesthetic of your venue. A selfie mirror makes a stunning addition to a modern or minimalist space, whilst a photo booth with a custom backdrop can complement any setting.

And remember — you do not have to choose just one. Many of our couples opt for two different entertainment options to keep guests entertained throughout the entire reception. Whatever you decide, your guests are guaranteed a brilliant time.

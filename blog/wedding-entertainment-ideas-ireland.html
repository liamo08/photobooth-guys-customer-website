Top 10 Wedding Entertainment Ideas in Ireland for 2026

Your wedding day is one of the most important celebrations of your life — and in 2026, Irish couples are going above and beyond to make sure their guests have an absolutely incredible time. Gone are the days when a DJ and a dancefloor were enough. Today's wedding entertainment ideas in Ireland are creative, interactive, and designed to create memories that last a lifetime.

Whether you are planning a grand celebration in a Dublin city centre hotel or an intimate gathering at a country estate in the midlands, the right entertainment can transform your reception from wonderful to truly unforgettable. Here are our top 10 fun wedding ideas for Ireland in 2026, based on what we are seeing couples book right now.

1. Photo Booth — The Classic Crowd-Pleaser

There is a reason the photo booth remains the single most popular wedding entertainment option in Ireland — it works brilliantly every single time. A modern open air photo booth brings professional DSLR photography, studio-quality lighting, a curated selection of props, and unlimited instant prints to your reception.

What makes a photo booth so special is its universality. Every guest — from your flower girl to your great-uncle — will have a go. The printed photos double as a fantastic guest book alternative, and you will receive a full digital gallery afterwards. It is effortless fun that keeps guests entertained for hours, particularly during those quieter moments between the meal and the first dance.

Our open air photo booth hire starts from €680, making it one of the most affordable entertainment options for the value it delivers.

2. 360 Video Booth — Viral Content for Your Big Day

The 360 video booth has exploded in popularity over the past two years, and it is easy to see why. Guests step onto a platform whilst a camera rotates around them, capturing stunning slow-motion video from every angle. The results are cinematic, shareable, and absolutely mesmerising.

What really sets the 360 booth apart is the social media factor. Your guests will receive their videos instantly via text or email, and within minutes those clips will be all over Instagram, TikTok, and WhatsApp groups. It is the ultimate way to generate buzz around your wedding and create content that your friends and family will watch on repeat. Our 360 booth hire starts from €750.

3. Selfie Mirror — Elegant Interactive Entertainment

For couples who want something a bit more sophisticated, the selfie mirror is a stunning choice. This full-length interactive mirror features animated prompts, touchscreen games, digital signatures, and beauty-enhancing filters — all wrapped in a sleek design that looks gorgeous in any venue.

The selfie mirror is particularly popular at elegant, black-tie weddings where couples want entertainment that complements rather than competes with the venue's aesthetic. Guests love the interactive element, and the instant prints are always a hit. Selfie mirror hire starts from €730.

4. Audio Phone Guestbook — Heartfelt Voice Messages

This is one of the most touching wedding entertainment ideas we have seen take off in Ireland. The audio phone guestbook features a beautiful vintage-style telephone that guests pick up to leave heartfelt voice messages for the happy couple.

Instead of a traditional paper guestbook that often sits in a drawer afterwards, you receive downloadable MP3 files of every single message. Imagine listening back to your grandparents' well-wishes, your best friend's hilarious anecdote, or your parents' emotional congratulations — years from now. It is genuinely one of the most meaningful keepsakes you can have from your wedding day, and it pairs perfectly alongside a photo booth for a complete entertainment package.

5. Live Music & Band

A brilliant live band remains the heartbeat of any great Irish wedding reception. There is nothing quite like a talented group of musicians reading the room and building the energy until every single guest is on the dancefloor. In 2026, we are seeing couples get creative with their music choices — think acoustic trios during the drinks reception, jazz quartets during dinner, and high-energy cover bands for the late-night party.

The best approach is to layer your musical entertainment throughout the day. An acoustic set during the champagne reception, a DJ for the early evening, and a full band to bring the house down after 10pm. Your guests will never have a dull moment.

6. Glam Photo Booth — Red Carpet Celebrity Portraits

If you want your guests to feel like absolute stars, the Glam Photo Booth is the ultimate choice. This premium booth delivers studio-quality portraits with professional lighting that makes every guest look like they belong on the cover of a magazine.

The Glam Booth is particularly popular with couples who want a touch of celebrity glamour at their wedding. The results are genuinely stunning — think Kardashian-style portraits with perfect lighting, flattering angles, and a polished finish that your guests will use as profile pictures for months afterwards. Glam Booth hire starts from €730.

7. Late-Night Food Stations

Nothing wins over wedding guests quite like unexpected food. Late-night food stations have become a staple of Irish weddings in 2026, and the options are endless — wood-fired pizza ovens, taco bars, gourmet burger stations, chip vans, crêpe stands, and even doughnut walls.

The key is timing. Set up your food station around 11pm, just when the dancing has really kicked off and guests are starting to work up an appetite again. It gives everyone a brilliant second wind and keeps the party going well into the early hours. Many Irish venues can arrange these directly, or you can hire specialist catering companies to set up on site.

8. Sparkler Send-Off

For a truly magical moment, a sparkler send-off is hard to beat. As the night draws to a close, your guests line up with lit sparklers to create a glittering tunnel for you and your new spouse to walk through. The photos and videos from a sparkler send-off are absolutely breathtaking — all those warm, golden lights against the night sky.

A word of practical advice: check with your venue first, as some indoor venues have restrictions on sparklers. Outdoor or semi-outdoor spaces work best. Long-handled sparklers (the 45cm variety) last longer and create a much more dramatic effect than the short ones. Hand them out with a lighter at each table towards the end of the night so everyone is ready to go.

9. Wedding Games — Lawn Games & Casino Tables

Interactive games are a fantastic way to break the ice and keep guests entertained during the drinks reception or between the ceremony and dinner. Giant Jenga, garden croquet, ring toss, and cornhole are all popular choices for outdoor celebrations. For evening entertainment, casino tables with a professional dealer add a touch of James Bond sophistication — think blackjack, roulette, and poker with play money.

The beauty of wedding games is that they naturally bring people together. Your college friends will end up chatting with your partner's work colleagues over a game of giant Connect Four, and before you know it everyone is getting on like a house on fire. It is the perfect icebreaker for weddings where two large social circles are meeting for the first time.

10. Roaming Photographer — Candid Captures with Instant Prints

Our roaming photographer is a newer concept that has quickly become one of our most popular services. Instead of guests coming to the entertainment, the entertainment comes to them. A professional photographer moves through your reception with a portable printer, capturing candid moments and delivering instant prints directly to your guests' hands.

The roaming photographer is brilliant for capturing those natural, unposed moments that a stationary booth simply cannot — the belly laughs at the table, the impromptu dance moves, the quiet conversations between old friends. Every guest receives a printed photo on the spot, and you get a complete digital gallery of beautifully candid shots from across the entire reception.

Tips for Combining Entertainment Options

The smartest approach to wedding entertainment in 2026 is layering different options throughout the day. Here is a combination that works brilliantly:

During the drinks reception: Lawn games and an acoustic musician keep guests relaxed and mingling. An audio phone guestbook can be set up from the very start, catching guests whilst their emotions are still running high from the ceremony.

After dinner: Set up the photo booth or selfie mirror. This is peak entertainment time — guests have had a few drinks, they are in great form, and the props box is calling. A 360 booth works fantastically during this window too.

Late night: The food station appears, the band is in full swing, and the roaming photographer is capturing all the magic. Close with a sparkler send-off for a picture-perfect ending.

Budget Planning: How to Allocate Your Entertainment Budget

Most Irish couples in 2026 are allocating between €2,000 and €5,000 for wedding entertainment beyond the band or DJ. Here is a rough guide to help you plan:

Essential tier (€680 - €1,000): One photo booth or selfie mirror covers your bases and guarantees a fantastic time for all guests. This is the best value-for-money entertainment investment you can make.

Enhanced tier (€1,200 - €2,000): Combine a photo booth with an audio guestbook, or pair a selfie mirror with a 360 booth. Two complementary options give guests variety and keep the entertainment fresh all evening.

Premium tier (€2,500+): Go all out with three or four entertainment options plus a late-night food station. At this level, your wedding becomes the event that everyone talks about for years.

Whatever your budget, the key is choosing entertainment that reflects your personalities as a couple and creates genuine moments of joy for your guests.
